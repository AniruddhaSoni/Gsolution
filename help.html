  • What is period poverty?

    Period poverty is a worldwide problem. It describes a lack of access to menstrual products, sanitation, and relevant information.

  • How should this application be used?

    This application has two end users. One is a needy person who is going through menstruation, and the other is a donor (such as a government organization, a non-profit organization, etc.) who is willing to help the needy by donating tampons, sanitary pads (soaps), and other necessary items.

  • How do I make a sanitary kit donation?

    On the map page, you can look for people in need (who you can help) near you. The red location icon on the map assists you in locating a needy person. Then you can click on the location icon, which will bring up a slide with all of the user's information, including their contact information. You may easily contact them and assist them there. It would be really appreciated if you could assist someone.

  • How can I get in touch with the organization/donor who is donating sanitary kits?

    Donor can be found on the map section. Navigate to the map and look for the donor, who is represented by the Tiffany blue colour location icon. Click on that icon to get all of the necessary information about the donor, including contact information.

  • How to help ending period poverty?

    Ending period poverty or period shame cannot begin and finish with simply giving period supplies such as pads and tampons; rather, it must begin with education and addressing discriminatory attitudes and beliefs.
